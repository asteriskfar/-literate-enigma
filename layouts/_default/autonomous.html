autonomous web & finance

The Signature Chain, or Sigchain, is a highly scalable Web3 and DeFi platform, with decentralised applications and payments built-in.

SIG marks a radical redesign of distributed ledger technology, by allowing each user to cryptographically sign their own personal blockchain. The Sigchain architecture gives users direct control of their identity, data, and financial relationships allowing them to create, trade and settle assets securely, and without intermediaries.


With a unique class of crypto-credit called Sigcoin, SIG offers the market an innovative way to create credit liquidity within business networks.

Sigcoin - Producer Credit

Sigcoin is a hybrid technology combining cryptographic components with producer credit. Sigchain does what neither banks nor legacy crypto can do, it enables direct peer-to-peer credit creation. It is the first digital currency to represent the trade credit relationship, thereby introducing scalable, trustful credit.

Reputable businesses can create new credit in the market itself to fund production. While producers can issue and extend credit to each other, and settle accounts, only those who honour their credit obligations will emerge as trusted liquidity providers.

Sigcoin aims to be a genuine 'stable coin' but rather than being backed by unstable fiat currency, Sigcoins are backed by the business networks that issue them. Beyond cryptocurrency, producer credit is the next evolution in decentralised finance.

Autonomous integration

Sigchain is the fusion of Web3, finance and market AI that introduces a new class of fully interoperable apps, with frictionless integration of payments into the entire application space.

Market participants are incentivised to build a reputable credit history on the network that will afford them the established trust and relationships they need to be successful. Consumers can determine if they want to interact based on this immutable evidence.

Earning reputation results in a system wide imperative to authenticate through network interactions. This market-based data provides validity of a persons identity without their data being owned by an intermediated controlling authority.

The next net

Web 2.0 requires web apps to be hosted on corporate owned servers where all of the users data is stored. In comparison, the definition of Web3 claims to give the user true ownership and control over their data. Yet Jack Dorsey and others raise questions about the viability of that vision.

Crypto and DeFi projects such as Ethereum and many more are pitched as fully decentralised Web3 networks, yet several centralised aspects of their implementations compromise their Web3 delivery. Dorsey acknowledges this hybridised reality when explaining his 'Web5' platform as a combination of Web3 and Web 2.0, built on the Bitcoin blockchain.

Kevin Werbach, author of The Blockchain and the New Architecture of Trust, also states that 'many so-called 'Web3' solutions are not as decentralised as they seem, while others have yet to show they are scalable, secure and accessible enough for the mass market’.

The notion that the blockchain can and will ‘decentralise’ everything and therefore give us back ownership and control of our identity and data is a misconception. The Ethereum blockchain, like any big database hosted on a server, is far too large to run on a local computer or phone, meaning only those that are incentivised, host the 'full nodes'. Users of crypto wallets, exchanges and platforms are required to access these nodes via 3rd party integrations and sharing of personal data.

SIGs entirely new, highly scalable and secure network architecture has solved the challenge of how to build a decentralised super-app platform with payments built-in. With near-zero infrastructure costs, Sigchain provides access to an autonomous marketplace that will introduce a paradigm shift in future application development and interaction.

Autonomous integration

As of May 2023, the total value locked in DeFi protocols was approximately $216 billion USD. While many of todays DeFi projects are speculating on tokens that are not backed by real-world assets, Sigchain connects markets to real production by trading SIG assets that compete to represent market exchange value.

The DeFi market includes financial applications such as decentralised exchanges, lending platforms, derivatives, and insurance. This industry has been enabled by the advent of blockchain technology, despite developers gaining the ability to exchange digital tokens, they still have no standard way to route credit to maintain the inherent credit relationship. Sigcoin accounts for the credit relationship, making it the worlds first digital currency to connect market data with real economic productivity.

Super Apps

Web apps today are siloed, they provide a fragmented experience and require heavy backend development to integrate 3rd party providers or make any meaningful improvements. On the Sigchain platform, trading, decentralised apps (Dapps), NFTs and crypto-credit are natively integrated. With a single-sign-on journey, users can access apps that seamlessly interoperate while retaining ownership and integrity over personal data. Being truly scalable with an unlimited transaction capacity, Sigchain also establishes a messaging and exchange layer that enables frictionless integration of payments into the entire application space.

As all transactions and messages are cryptographically signed, Sigchain is essentially an NFT engine that does not require an external wallet or 3rd party integrations. Users will be able to produce, collect and trade NFTs and coins within an intuitive and personally curated social ecosystem. The value of these digital assets is decided by the consumers, based on the reputation of the creator. Because the account and transaction graph is a structured data set, users can see which producers are popular and credible or new and emerging.
